Participation and Optimism

"Participation and Optimism in the Political Context: How Optimism Both Helps and Hinders Political Participation" is my senior thesis in political psychology. It was found that optimism did not provide a general resiliency against feelings of electoral loss as expected, but instead functioned as a moderating variable that amplified pre-existing positive or negative effects. Special attention to specific measures of optimism in political science, including a custom political optimism battery, is also discussed.

Different Hearts for Different Heads

"Different Hearts for Different Heads: How Personality Moderates the Emotional Impact of Political Advertisement" is original political psychology research exploring differences in ad perception across all of the Big Five personality factors, using a complex survey-embedded radio ad experiment.

The Employer of Last Resort

"The Employer of Last Resort: A Policy to Ensure Full Employment and Greater Price Stability" is original economics research looking at acheiving truly full employment and price stability via a government Employer of Last Resort program.

New Answers to New Threats

"New Answers to New Threats: Foreign Aid Expansion as a Response to September 11" is original foreign policy research using counterfactual analysis that claims the expansion of foreign aid in the George W. Bush administration was connected to acheiving post-9/11 foreign policy objectives.
